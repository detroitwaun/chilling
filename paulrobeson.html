Paul Robeson

Paul Leroy Robeson was born on April 9, 1898. Paul was an American bass singer and actor who participated in the Civil Rights Movement.

Paul was born in Princeton, New Jersey, in 1898. Paul Robeson attended Rutgers University in New Brunswick. Paul Robeson was an football player who played for the Akron Pros. He went to law school in 1919 and graduated in 1922, he attended Columbia Law School. Paul was born to William Robeson & Robeson. Paul rejected the career as a professional athlete and went onto Columbia Law School. He secured a law degree but since the racism and lack of African American opportunity, he went on to his acting career and made his debut in London and went on and joined a group named ProvinceTown Players. He starred in his first film in 1933 named The Emperor Jones. Paul had the lead role. As Paul started traveling more he started to understand that racism was not as strong in Europe than in the United States. At home where Paul lived, it was hard for him to find restaurants that would serve him. In New York blacks had to sit in the upper balcony and performances was often boo-ed and harassed but in Europe he brought the whole audience to their feet.

Paul Robeson spoke about racism and injustice often. He supported Pan Africanism and sang for the loyalist soldiers during Spain's Civil War. Also he took part in anti- Nazi demonstrations.Back in his birth place he had trouble with the government but still got credited for his phenomenal Broadway production but while struggling with the government. While the government was looking for him to be silence, a man that spoke out against the civil rights and racism was not being silent. In 1958, he published his book, Here I Stand, that same year he got his passport reinstated. Paul Robeson published a lot of books like, The Black Way of Seeing, The Undiscovered Paul Robeson, Quest for Freedom, and Paul Robeson Jr. Speaks to America. Paul also had a wife named Eslanda Goode Robeson and one son named Paul Robeson Jr. Paul was married to Eslanda for 44 years. Maria Louisa Bustill Robeson was a Quaker schoolteacher; the wife of the Reverend William Drew Robeson of Witherspoon Street Presbyterian Church in Princeton, New Jersey and the mother of Paul Robeson and his siblings. Paul Robeson had four other siblings named Marian Robeson, Benjamin Robeson, Reeve Robeson, and William Drew Robeson Jr. William Drew Robeson I was the father of Paul Robeson and the minister of Witherspoon Street Presbyterian Church in Princeton, New Jersey from 1880 to 1901. Paul Robeson was a very creative and smart man, he spoke twenty different languages. Sadly after returning home to his family almost a decade later paul robeson died because of a stroke, January 23, 1976.
